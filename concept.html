Overview

We’re building a shared infrastructure for training resources: data, evals, labeling, and compute. Model builders post needs with a budget; contributors submit resources with documentation and licenses; measurable improvements trigger bounty payouts. Public rules, reproducible pipelines, and auditable logs keep it fair.

Problem

  • High-quality resources are siloed, expensive, or locked behind NDAs.
  • Teams reinvent datasets and evals instead of reusing and improving them.
  • Incentives favor hype over measurable, reproducible gains.

Solution

A community marketplace with clear specs, quality gates, and impact-based payouts. Contributors get credit and rewards when their work moves the needle; requesters get vetted, documented resources that are easy to integrate.

  • Open specs: schemas, data cards, and licensing guidance.
  • Quality gates: provenance, documentation, deduplication, privacy/ethics checks.
  • Impact: before/after metrics on public eval suites, with reproducible scripts.

How it Works

  1. Post a bounty: Scope, budget, deadline, quality gates, target metric, license.
  2. Contribute: Submit dataset/eval/labels/compute + docs. Early sample encouraged.
  3. Evaluate: Run standardized pipeline; compare metrics; check regressions and data health.
  4. Payout + attribution: Fixed, tiered, or leaderboard rewards. Public credit optional/pseudonymous.
See Bounty Design

Bounty Design

Each bounty includes:

  • Scope (resource type, domain, size, format)
  • Quality gates (schema, docs, provenance, licensing, privacy, ethics)
  • Impact metric (benchmarks + required delta, e.g., +2.0 F1 without regressions)
  • Payout rules (fixed/tiered/leaderboard; escrowed funds; review SLA)
  • Licensing (CC-BY/SA, OpenRAIL, dual-license options)

Anti-gaming: deduplication, hidden holdouts, hash checks, red-team reviews, poisoning detection.

Governance

  • Public ledger of bounties, submissions, decisions, and payouts.
  • Reproducibility: containerized evals (Docker), seeds, and scripts published.
  • Appeals: one-round appeal judged by a rotating panel.
  • Attribution: contributor name/org/pseudonym; optional anonymity.

Roadmap

  • Phase 1: Specs, sample bounties, manual review pipeline.
  • Phase 2: Self-serve posting, submission portal, basic analytics.
  • Phase 3: Automated eval runners, dedup scanning, public dashboards.

FAQ

Is this open source? Core specs and sample pipelines will be public; platform code TBD.

Can I use sensitive data? Only with explicit consent, compliant licenses, and privacy safeguards. Otherwise, no PII.

How do you prevent overfitting? Hidden holdouts, periodic benchmark refreshes, and cross-bench validation.

Who funds the bounties? Requesting teams, grants, and sponsors. Funds are escrowed on posting.
